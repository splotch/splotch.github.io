A document that smells like jelly

When you have a document, you don't typically want it to smell like jelly. But if that document needs to be eaten by a small child, then perhaps it wouldn't be a bad thing that it smelled like something sweet and sticky and delicious. Of course, why would you have a document that smells like jelly in the first place? And that is a question for another paragraph.

Now let us explore the conundrum of why one would ever find themselves with a document that smells like jelly. The obvious answer would be that their document had an unfortunate encounter with the wrong side of a jelly covered slice of toast. Or perhaps a toddler was enjoying some figurepainting with a jar of jelly rather than the usual fingerpainting medium of paint. Does it really matter?
